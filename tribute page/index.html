SIR ISAAC NEWTON

1643-1727

Father of Physics

SIR ISAAC NEWTON , born on January 4, 1643, in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England, is renowned as one of the most influential scientists in history. He laid the groundwork for classical mechanics and made significant contributions to mathematics and optics. Newton's seminal work, "Philosophi√¶ Naturalis Principia Mathematica," published in 1687, introduced his laws of motion and the law of universal gravitation. These laws provided a comprehensive framework for understanding the motion of celestial bodies and objects on Earth, unifying terrestrial and celestial mechanics. In mathematics, Newton developed calculus independently around the same time as Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz. His work in optics included experiments with prisms and the discovery that white light is composed of a spectrum of colors. Newton served as the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge and was knighted in 1705. His profound impact on science and mathematics has earned him the title "Father of Physics" and solidified his legacy as a key figure in the Scientific Revolution. Newton passed away on March 31, 1727, in London.
